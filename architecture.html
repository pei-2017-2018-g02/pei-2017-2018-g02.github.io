ARCHITECTURE

All the hardware components and wiring will be hidden inside the table, with the exception, of course, of the smart light.

 

There will be 4 piezos that will detect impacts on top of the table, and by placing one in each corner it will make it possible to triangulate where exactly was the impact.

 

A bone conductor, will be the audio output, it turns ordinary objects into speakers by vibrating them.

 

Nine LEDs, one for each square of the 3 by 3 grid, which will create an effect of a colored square.

 

A smart light used for regulating the feeling of the room, unlike the other components this one won’t be physically connected to the table, but instead will be controlled wirelessly.

 

And finally a Raspberry Pi, this component will be the central piece that connects everything, it will collect and process the data received by the piezos, as well as all the output such as the LEDs, the smart light, and the bone conductor.

 

​Note: only 4 piezos were used because the ADC only supports 4, and the Raspberry Pi only has place for 1 ADC.
